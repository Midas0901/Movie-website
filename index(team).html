DATA TRENDS

A summary of the biggest changes in genre popularity, studio dominance, and audience behavior from 2010 to 2025.

🦸

Superhero Expansion

Marvel and DC shaped much of the blockbuster market during the 2010s, pushing action and superhero films into global dominance.

🦠

Pandemic Disruption

The 2020 theatrical collapse caused a dramatic fall in box office revenue, interrupting long-term growth across nearly all genres.

📺

Streaming Shift

Audience habits increasingly moved toward digital viewing, changing how theatrical releases performed and how success was measured.

Interpretation

Overall, the film industry between 2010 and 2025 shows a strong rise in franchise-based entertainment, followed by a major disruption during the pandemic period and a gradual transformation caused by streaming platforms. While blockbuster studios such as Marvel remained powerful in revenue generation, companies like Pixar often showed stronger long-term critical consistency. This suggests that financial success and audience or critic approval do not always move in exactly the same direction.
